33 /// Mangles @p s into a dense 48-bit representation.
34 /// The layout is as follows:
35 /// ```
36 /// |---7--||---6--||---5--||---4--||---3--||---2--||---1--||---0--|
37 /// 7654321076543210765432107654321076543210765432107654321076543210
38 /// Char67Char66Char65Char64Char63Char62Char61Char60|---reserved---|
39 /// ```
40 /// The `reserved` part is used for the Axiom::tag and the Axiom::sub.
41 /// Each `Char6x` is 6-bit wide and hence a dialect name has at most Axiom::Max_Dialect_Size = 8 chars.
42 /// It uses this encoding:
43 /// | `Char6` | ASCII |
44 /// |---------|---------|
45 /// | 1: | `_` |
46 /// | 2-27: | `a`-`z` |
47 /// | 28-53: | `A`-`Z` |
48 /// | 54-63: | `0`-`9` |
49 /// The 0 is special and marks the end of the name if the name has less than 8 chars.
50 /// @returns `std::nullopt` if encoding is not possible.
