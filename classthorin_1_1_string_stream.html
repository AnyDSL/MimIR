template<class T , class... Args>
Stream & thorin::Stream::fmt ( const char *  s,
T &&  t,
Args &&...  args 
)
inherited

fprintf-like function. Each "{}" in s corresponds to one of the variadic arguments in args. The type of the corresponding argument must support operator<< on std::ostream& or preferably Stream. Furthermore, an argument may also be a range-based container where its elements fulfill the condition above. You can specify the separator within the braces:

s.fmt("({, })", list) // yields "(a, b, c)"

If you use "\n" as separator, it will invoke Stream::endl - keeping indentation:

s.fmt("({\n})", list)

Finally, you can use "\n", "\t", and "\b" to endl, indent, or dedent, respectively.
