71/// Performs *typed closure conversion*.
72/// This is based on the [Simply Typed Closure Conversion](https://dl.acm.org/doi/abs/10.1145/237721.237791).
73/// Closures are represented using dependent pairs `[env_type:*, cn[env_type, Args..], env_type]`.
74/// In general only *continuations* are converted (i.e Lam%s that have type `cn[...]`).
75/// Different kind of Lam%s may be rewritten differently:
76/// - *returning continuations* ("functions"), *join-points* and *branches* are fully closure converted.
77/// - *return continuations* are not closure converted.
78/// - *first-class continuations* get a "dummy" closure, they still have free variables.
79///
80/// This pass relies on ClosConvPrep to introduce annotations for these cases.
81///
82/// Note: Since direct-style Def%s are not rewritten, this can lead to problems with certain Axiom%s:
83/// `ax : (B : *, int -> B) -> (int -> B)` won't be converted, possible arguments may.
84/// Further, there is no machinery to handle free variables in a Lam%s type; this may lead to
85/// problems with polymorphic functions.
86class ClosConv {
87public:
88 ClosConv(World& world)
89 : world_(world)
90 , fva_(world)
91 , closures_()
92 , glob_noms_()
93 , worklist_(){};
